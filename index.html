An Ode to Zucchini Bread with Recipe

Zucchini bread can be enjoyed as a breakfast item, snack, or dessert. It's often served sliced and can be eaten plain or spread with butter, cream cheese, or a drizzle of honey. The moist and slightly sweet nature of zucchini bread makes it a favorite among those who love a touch of homemade comfort in their baked goods.

I was first introduced to zucchini bread in high school. I had a friend who use to work at a resturant in Selma, Alabama that goes by the name Tally-Ho. One day he brought some zucchini bread to school for us to try. I'm a foodie, but squash was a no no for me during this time and mixed in with my favorite food - bread? IMMEDIATELY no. However, after trying it, I actually enjoyed it and it became something I always enjoyed having. I loved it so much at the time, my twin sister and I wrote a poem as a part of one of our english projects.

An Ode...

Oh, zucchini bread, a garden's treasure found, In your tender crumb, delights abound. From humble squash to culinary grace, You bring a smile to every hungry face.

A summer's harvest, fresh and green, In your embrace, a world unseen. Grated zucchini, you blend so fine, In every slice, your magic doth shine.

With cinnamon's warmth and nutmeg's spice, You offer comfort, you taste so nice. Eggs and sugar, in perfect blend, Create a journey from end to end.

In the oven's heat, you rise and grow, A fragrant aroma in every flow. Golden crust, a promise to keep, In your depths, secrets to reap.

Optional add-ins, a creative spree, Nuts or chocolate, or fruits to see. Each baker's touch, a unique stroke, In your recipe, a world bespoke.

Morning's delight, or a midnight snack, Zucchini bread, there's nothing you lack. With tea or coffee, you steal the show, A taste of heaven, to all you bestow.

You teach us a lesson, oh bread so fine, From the garden's bounty, divine design. A lesson of transformation, you've taught, From simple zucchini, true art is wrought.

So, here's to you, zucchini bread so dear, In every season, you bring us cheer. An ode to your flavor, your humble grace, In zucchini bread, we've found our place.

Ingredients

  • 1 and 3/4 cups (220g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) vegetable oil (or melted coconut oil)
  • 1/2 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup (60g) unsweetened applesauce (or sour cream or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 and 1/2 cups (180g) shredded zucchini (no need to blot)
  • optional: 1 cup (180g) semi-sweet chocolate chips (or chopped nuts, raisins, etc)

Optional Ingredients

Zucchini bread is a wonderful way to enjoy the mild and slightly sweet flavor of zucchini in a baked treat. It's a versatile recipe that can be adapted to your preferences and is perfect for breakfast, brunch, or as a sweet snack. It can be customized to suit your taste. You can add other mix-ins like raisins, dried cranberries, or shredded coconut. Some recipes also call for the inclusion of lemon zest for a citrusy twist.

Here are a few popular variations and optional ingredients you can add to your zucchini bread:

Nuts: Chopped nuts, such as walnuts, pecans, or almonds, add a delightful crunch and nutty flavor to the bread. They are a common and classic addition.

Dried Fruits: Dried fruits like raisins, currants, cranberries, or chopped dried apricots can provide bursts of sweetness and a chewy texture.

Whole Grains: For a healthier twist, you can replace some of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour, oat flour, or other whole grains to increase the fiber content.

Yogurt or Sour Cream: Incorporating yogurt or sour cream can make the zucchini bread even more moist and tender while imparting a subtle tangy taste

Caramel Bits: Incorporating caramel bits into the batter can result in a sweet, gooey surprise with each bite.

Pumpkin Puree: Combining zucchini with pumpkin puree can create a unique flavor and texture combination, perfect for the fall season.

Remember that while these optional ingredients can add depth and variety to your zucchini bread, it's essential to balance the quantities to maintain the bread's structure and texture. Be creative and experiment to find your favorite zucchini bread variation, and enjoy the delightful results!

Cooking Instructions

  1. Start by preheating your oven to a specific temperature, usually around 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grate the zucchini and then squeeze out excess moisture using a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine all wet ingredients - grated zucchini, sugar, eggs, and oil or melted butter. Mix until well combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients, including the flour, baking powder, baking soda, etc. and spices.
  5. Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as it can make the bread dense.
  6. If desired, fold in the optional mix-ins, such as chopped nuts or chocolate chips.
  7. Pour the batter into a greased and floured loaf pan.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for about 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow the zucchini bread to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.
